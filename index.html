A hot wallet, such as a software or online wallet, is connected to the internet and is more susceptible to hacking. In contrast, a hardware/cold wallet is offline when not in use, making it immune to online threats. Hardware/cold wallets are considered 'cold storage,' providing a higher level of security for long-term storage of cryptocurrencies

Using a hardware/cold wallet involves setting it up, generating a seed phrase (recovery phrase), and securely storing it. You can then send and receive cryptocurrencies through the wallet's interface. Transactions are signed within the device, ensuring your private keys never leave the secure environment of the hardware/cold wallet
